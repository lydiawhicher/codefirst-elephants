There are two distinct species of African elephant: the forest elephant and the savannah elephant. These two species have many physical differences and they live in vastly different habitats, but both are known for their intellience and sensitivity - including their ability to recognize themselves in the mirror & mourn their dead - and they are both keystone species, meaning they play critical roles in their ecosystems.

LOXODONTA AFRICANA (African savannah)

Status: Vulnerable
Height: Shoulder height is 12 feet
Weight: 6 tons
Habitats: Tropical and Subtropical Moist Broadleaf Forests, Flooded Grasslands and Savannahs, Miombo woodlands, Acacia savannahs

LOXODONTA CYCLOTIS (African forest)

Status: Vulnerable
Height: Shoulder height is 8 feet
Weight: 2.7 tons
Habitats: Forests of the Congo Basin in Central Africa
